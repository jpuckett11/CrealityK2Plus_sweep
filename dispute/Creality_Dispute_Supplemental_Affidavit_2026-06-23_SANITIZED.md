## 1. Purpose of this supplemental statement

I, Jay Puckett, am the cardholder of record for the transaction currently
in dispute with Creality. This supplemental affidavit documents
developments in the dispute that occurred after the date of my original
2026-06-17 affidavit. The new developments concern the merchant's
attempted redirection of remediation through a third-party insurance
program (Seel Worry-Free Purchase) and the merchant's request for direct
banking-channel transfer information (ACH, wire, or SWIFT) in lieu of
normal card-network refund processing.

I am submitting this supplemental statement under penalty of perjury, in
continued support of the chargeback already initiated with my bank, and
in order to put on the record the merchant conduct that has occurred
since my original affidavit was signed.

The chargeback dispute with my bank remains active. Nothing in this
supplemental affidavit should be construed as withdrawal, modification,
or concession of any element of the original dispute.

## 2. Timeline of post-2026-06-17 merchant communications

On a date after 2026-06-17 and on or about 2026-06-22, I received a
written communication from Creality customer service stating, in
substance: "Hello, we have contacted Seel Insurance to extend your case.
Please submit your claim as soon as possible. If you have any further
questions, please contact us. Thank you for your understanding!"

This communication was received during the period when the chargeback
dispute was already active with my bank. Creality did not, in this
communication or in any subsequent communication of which I am aware,
substantively engage with the underlying dispute (the substitution of
routine printer consumables for the represented Creality CR-Scan Otter
3D scanner). Creality did not produce the evidentiary records the
bank's dispute process can compel, as enumerated in Section 5 of my
original 2026-06-17 affidavit (carrier weight and dimension records for
each of the three shipped packages; proof of delivery; internal
warehouse pick list).

Instead, Creality redirected the matter to Seel, Inc., a third-party
"Worry-Free Purchase" insurance-program provider, and instructed me to
file a claim through Seel.

Shortly thereafter, in continued correspondence regarding refund
processing, Creality requested that I provide direct banking-channel
transfer information (ACH, wire, or SWIFT) for the purpose of receiving
a refund. The request was for routing and account information
sufficient to initiate a direct bank deposit, outside the original
card-network payment channel through which the disputed transaction was
funded.

## 3. The Seel Worry-Free Purchase coverage and its documented timing limitation

Seel Worry-Free Purchase, per the public terms of service published at
`seel.com`, is an opt-in protection program that the customer must
elect at the point of purchase. The coverage has documented time limits
on claim filing for theft and porch-piracy cases.

I have personal knowledge that the standard 7-day porch-piracy claim
window described in Seel's program terms had elapsed by the time
Creality first raised Seel coverage as a possible remediation path.
Seel's program terms, the timing of Creality's first reference to Seel
coverage in my case, and my own subsequent communications with Seel
regarding the claim documentation requirements are all matters for
which I have either documentary evidence or knowledge from direct
experience that I am willing to substantiate to the bank.

When I previously attempted to engage with Seel during the period of
the chargeback dispute, Seel's documentation requirements for the
theft / porch-piracy claim category included a police report. Local
law enforcement declined to write a police report on the underlying
package issue, for reasons including: the package was a part of the
original Creality order rather than a separate replacement; I had no
prior possession of the package; the carrier identified on the
tracking was an off-brand courier rather than a major carrier with
verifiable delivery infrastructure; and no theft was witnessed because
I never had the package in my possession at any point.

Creality's 2026-06-22 communication asserts that Seel has agreed to
"extend" the case. I will submit a claim per Creality's instruction,
in good faith and on the documentary record I have. I have no reason to
believe Seel's documentation requirements have been relaxed, and I
expect denial or extended-pendency status for the same documentation
reasons that previously limited my ability to pursue the Seel pathway.

## 4. Documented Seel claim-handling concerns relevant to the bank's dispute

In the period between the original 2026-06-17 affidavit and the present
supplemental affidavit, I have reviewed publicly-available consumer
complaint records concerning Seel, Inc.'s handling of porch-piracy and
non-delivery claims. The pattern relevant to the bank's evaluation of
this dispute includes:

consumer review on Sitejabber documents an instance in which Seel
reported a refund as processed via Venmo and provided a transaction
ID. The consumer contacted Venmo to verify the transaction. Venmo
confirmed that the transaction ID was not associated with any actual
Venmo transaction. When the consumer presented this to Seel, Seel
revised their position to state that the transfer "had not gone
through." The pattern documented in this consumer's experience is
consistent with the practice of providing fabricated transaction
confirmations to consumers in order to cause those consumers to drop
or withdraw pending chargeback disputes. The original consumer
account is publicly readable at `sitejabber.com/reviews/seel.com`.

**Documented claim-process biometric demands.** Publicly-posted
consumer complaints document instances in which Seel required a face
scan from the claimant as a precondition for claim adjudication, with
refusal of the biometric requirement resulting in claim closure

## 5. The merchant's request for direct ACH, wire, or SWIFT transfer information

Separately from the Seel redirection, Creality has requested that I
provide ACH, wire, or SWIFT transfer information for direct deposit of
a refund. I have declined this request and have so informed the
merchant in writing.

I am informed and believe that, under card-network rules and standard
merchant-card-processing practice, refunds for a card-funded purchase
must be processed back through the original payment method. The
merchant's request for a separate banking-channel deposit pathway is
not consistent with standard card-network refund handling.

The combination of:

(a) the merchant's redirection of the case to a third-party insurance
program with documented chargeback-collision and fake-confirmation
patterns; and

(b) the merchant's separate request for direct banking-channel transfer
information outside the card-network payment rail; and

(c) the fact that both moves occurred during the active period of the
chargeback dispute with my bank;

is, in my belief and in my experience, consistent with a coordinated
attempt to defeat the active chargeback rather than to genuinely
remediate the underlying dispute. I respectfully submit this
observation to the bank for the bank's own evaluation under its
dispute-process procedures.

I have not provided, and will not provide, direct banking-channel
transfer information to Creality. I have not withdrawn, and will not
withdraw, the chargeback dispute. I have not accepted, and will not
accept, any refund-confirmation message from Creality or Seel as
evidence of refund unless and until the actual receipt of funds is
verified through my bank account or through the original card payment
channel.
